## Data Authority and Limitations

### Source Authority

This Tool aggregates data from official Latvian legal databases. However:

| Source Type | Authority Level | Use Case |
|------------|----------------|----------|
| **likumi.lv** | Authoritative | Statute text, consolidated legislation (primary reference) |
| **latvijas vestnesis (vestnesis.lv)** | Authoritative | Official Gazette — legislation as enacted |
| **Community/open-data sources** | Supplementary | Cross-references, metadata (verify independently) |
| **EUR-Lex / treaty databases** | Authoritative for EU/international law | EU directives, regulations, international obligations |

**CRITICAL**: Community-maintained resources are **NOT** official government publications. While generally reliable, they may contain transcription errors, outdated information, or incomplete coverage.

### Commercial Legal Databases

For professional legal work, **always cross-check with commercial legal databases** such as:

- LVKIS (Latvijas Vēstneša tiesību informācijas sistēma), Jurista vārds

These services provide editorial oversight, comprehensive annotations (komentāri), and professional-grade currency guarantees that this Tool **cannot** provide.

## Currency and Staleness

### No Real-Time Updates

- Database updates are **manual** and may lag official publications by **weeks or months**
- Latvian law changes continuously through new laws (likumi), regulations (noteikumi), amendments (grozījumi), and court decisions (tiesas spriedumi)
- **Last-updated timestamps** in tool responses indicate data age, but should be treated as unreliable

### Staleness Warnings

The Tool includes staleness warnings when data is >30 days old. **These warnings are not exhaustive**:

- Even "fresh" data may be outdated if recent amendments were published in the Latvijas Vēstnesis (Official Gazette)
- Staleness warnings do not guarantee completeness or accuracy
- Always check official sources for the current legal position (spēkā esošais tiesiskais regulējums)

### Amendments and Repeals

- A provision marked "in force" may have been:
  - Amended (grozīts) after the last database update
  - Repealed (atcelts) but not yet removed from the database
  - Subject to transitional provisions (pārejas noteikumi) affecting its applicability
- The Tool does **NOT** automatically track amendments in real-time

## Coverage Gaps

### What's Missing

This Tool does **NOT** include:

1. **Complete case law** — Coverage of Augstākā tiesa (Supreme Court), Satversmes tiesa (Constitutional Court), and lower court decisions is limited; do not rely solely on this for case law research (tiesu prakses izpēte)
2. **Historical statute versions** — Limited availability of historical provision wording (vēsturiskās redakcijas)
3. **Legal commentary** — No editorial annotations (komentāri), academic commentary (tiesību zinātne), or practice guides
4. **Unpublished decisions** — Lower court decisions and administrative decisions (administratīvie lēmumi) largely absent
5. **Full legislative history** — Limited coverage of parliamentary materials (Saeimas likumprojekti, anotācijas, stenogrammas)

### Impact on Professional Use

These gaps mean:

- **Incomplete Legal Research**: Tool results are inherently incomplete and may miss critical authorities
- **Context Missing**: Without commentary (komentārs) and legislative materials, interpretation may be incorrect
- **International Law Blind Spots**: Cannot fully assess EU law obligations or international treaty compliance
